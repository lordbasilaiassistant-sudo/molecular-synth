## 1. Order (see /bom for live links + prices)
1. **Scaffold ssDNA** — M13mp18 (e.g. NEB N4040 M13mp18), resuspend/quantify to a known nM.
2. **Staple oligos** — order the 20 sequences in `staples.csv`. For ~200 sequences,
   IDT **oPools** (pooled synthesis) is far cheaper per base than a 96-well plate; either works.
3. **Folding buffer (FOB)** — 1× TAE + **12.5 mM MgCl₂** (40 mM Tris, 20 mM acetic acid,
   1 mM EDTA, 12.5 mM MgCl₂). Nuclease-free water throughout.

## 2. Assemble the folding reaction (50 µL)

| Scaffold (M13mp18) | 20 nM |

## 4. Verify the fold (DIY agarose gel — hardware/cad/gel_box)
1. Cast a **1.5–2 % agarose** gel in 1× TAE + 12.5 mM MgCl₂ (Mg in the *gel and running
   buffer* keeps origami intact).
2. Load 10 µL folding reaction + loading dye; include a DNA ladder and a scaffold-only lane.
3. Run **~60–90 V, 1.5–3 h**, gel box on ice or in a cold room (heat denatures origami).
4. Stain with GelRed/SYBR Safe; image on the blue-LED transilluminator.
   **Well-folded origami runs as one tight band migrating differently from the scaffold-only
   lane**; smearing/ladders = misfolding or aggregation → slow the ramp or retune Mg.

## 5. (Optional) Purify
PEG precipitation or a 100 kDa spin filter removes excess staples before hardening/imaging.

## 6. (Optional) Harden — DNA → silica (sol–gel)
To convert the soft template into a rigid silica replica (Liu 2018 Nature; Nguyen 2019
Angew. Chem.): incubate purified origami with an aminosilane (TMAPS/APTES) then TEOS in
the Mg buffer for ~1–4 days at room temperature; the prehydrolysed silica condenses on the
DNA backbone. **Do this in a fume hood** (TEOS/alcohol). This is the demonstrated route to a
mechanically robust copy. ALD metal-oxide coating (TiO₂/ZnO/Al₂O₃) is an alternative but
needs a vacuum reactor — out of the desktop budget; treat as a service step.

## 7. (Optional) Atomic-resolution check
The DIY STM (hardware/cad/stm) resolves the atomic lattice of HOPG graphite — it is the rig's
atomic-resolution *capability demonstrator*, not a direct origami imager (soft, hydrated DNA
is not STM-friendly). Use the gel for routine origami QC.
